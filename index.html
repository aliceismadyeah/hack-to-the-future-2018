July 17th - 18th

What can you build in 24 hours?

Register Your Team »

A hack is quick, cheap, and rarely pretty. But like duct tape, a hack does the job. Sometimes "hack" refers to a piece of code, but it can be any quick solution to a problem. In this spirit, we present Hack to the Future. This is your moment to flex your creativity and pour your energy into making something better.

We're setting aside a day for your own personal projects, where you can turn your ideas into tangible presentations. The only guideline is that your project must make our company's products or services better. Your idea might be a new site feature, a new marketing campaign, a new incentive for contributors, or a new way to make the company operate more smoothly.

All employees at Shutterstock and Bigstock are invited to participate, and we want you to work in teams. We'll unveil your projects at a special off-site presentation.

A recommended team size is 4 to 5 people, but you can recruit as many people for your team as you like, and you can be on multiple teams. To be eligible to present and win prizes, your team must include employees from at least 3 departments, and even more are encouraged. For this event, departments are thought of as Tech, Marketing, Product, Content Ops, Customer Management, Operations (Finance, Legal, HR), Sales, & International.

You can start brainstorming ideas, assembling your team and working on your project as soon as you like. We'd like you to post the names of your team members with an original team name and brief project description to the Team Registration page.

We will also be holding an information session the week of July 15 and preparing you through our Hackademy sessions!

If you have further questions, please visit see our FAQ Page.

We can't wait to see what you create!

FAQ

Logistics

Does this run all night?
Hack to the Future runs 24 hours from noon to noon. The office is open all night, and you can sleep if you want! Project demo presentations will then take place during the afternoon of Friday, July 17.


Do we have to work through the entire 24 hour period?
No, you can work on your project as much as you want.


Will you provide snacks and refreshments?
Yes!


Is it mandatory?
No, but we strongly encourage everyone to participate.


What if my regular job duties prevent me from participating?
Please talk to your manager to see if together you can make it work. You may participate for just part of the day if that works better.


Does my project have to "work?"
No, you can have your project as realized as you want it to be, from an initial comp to a fully working prototype.


Can I work from home/out of office? Yes, but only with the approval of your manager.


Projects & Teams

How will the company use my project or idea?
The projects will be showcased on this site post-event. We will refer to these when thinking of the product roadmap or new company initiatives.


What are some examples of projects?
New site features for customers or contributors
New marketing ideas, including swag and giveaways
New social media concepts


Does my project have to be technical or related to the web site?
No, your project can be anything as long as it "makes our company's products or services better."


What if there are no programmers on my team?
Attend Hackademy! It's a series of basic programming courses taught by your lovely co-workers.


How will I know if my idea has been hacked together already?
Check out ideas from 2011, 2012. Feel free to iterate on anything you see!


Are Shutterstock logos, buttons, and other design assets available for me to use in my project?
Yes! See the Design Assets link in the tab bar above or go to this page (works from inside the office or on the VPN).


Demo Presentations & Prizes

What teams will be eligible to present and to win prizes?
Only teams with people from at least 3 different departments will be eligible to present and to win prizes. Solo hackers and teams with members from fewer departments are still welcome to hack.


What are the different departments at Shutterstock?
For this event, departments are thought of as Tech, Marketing, Product, Content Ops, Customer Management, Operations (Finance, Legal, HR), Sales, & International.


How does my team get to demo?
If your team has people from at least 3 different departments, fill out the Team Registration form for presentation slot.


If I register my team early, will I get to present early during the demo event?
Not necessarily. Demo presentation order might be rearranged from team sign up order.


How much time will I have to present my Hack?
Each team will have a limited amount of time to present their hack project. (Stay tuned for an actual number.) You are strongly encouraged to practice this presentation.


Will there be prizes?
Each hack will be judged during the off-side Demos according to different categories. Winners of each category will be recognized in some size, shape or form.
