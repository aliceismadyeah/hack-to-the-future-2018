FAQ

Logistics

Does this run all night?
Hack to the Future runs 24 hours from noon to noon. The office is open all night, and you can sleep if you want! Project demo presentations will then take place during the afternoon of Friday, July 18th.


Do we have to work through the entire 24 hour period?
No, you can work on your project as much as you want.


Will you provide snacks and refreshments?
Yes! We'll be serving lunch, dinner, breakfast, and snacks during the 24 hour event.


Is it mandatory?
No, but we strongly encourage everyone to participate.


What if my regular job duties prevent me from participating?
Please talk to your manager to see if together you can make it work. You may participate for just part of the day if that works better.


Does my project have to "work?"
No, you can have your project as realized as you want it to be, from an initial comp to a fully working prototype.


Can I work from home/out of office?
Yes, but only with the approval of your manager.


Projects & Teams

How will the company use my project or idea?
The projects will be showcased on this site post-event. We will refer to these when thinking of the product roadmap or new company initiatives.


What are some examples of projects?
New site features for customers or contributors
New marketing ideas, including swag and giveaways
New social media concepts
Internal tools to help Shutterstock employees
Sales call-a-tho


Does my project have to be technical or related to the web site?
No, your project can be anything as long as it "makes our company's products or services better."


What if there are no programmers on my team?
Attend Hackademy! You don't need to know coding to build a winning demo presentation. Get ready for hands on workshops to learn how to detail out your Hackathon idea, build interactive mockups that look great and present your idea clearly.


How will I know if my idea has been hacked together already?
Check out ideas from 2011, 2012, and 2013. Feel free to iterate on anything you see!


Are Shutterstock logos, buttons, and other design assets available for me to use in my project?
Yes! You can get design assets by going to this page (works from inside the office or on the VPN).


Demo Presentations & Prizes

What teams will be eligible to present and to win prizes?
Only teams with people from at least 3 different departments will be eligible to present and to win prizes. Solo hackers and teams with members from fewer departments are still welcome to hack.


What are the different departments at Shutterstock?
For this year, departments are defined as:
Tech (Engineers / Tech Ops / QA / BI)
Marketing (Regional Directors / Marketing Design / Blog / PR / Email / Events)
Product (Product Managers / UX Designers / Researchers / GMs)
Content (Reviewers / Researchers / Editors / Contributor Support)
Sales (AE / AM / Sales)
Operations (Finance / Legal / HR / Biz Dev)


How does my team get to demo?
If your team has people from at least 3 different departments, fill out the Team Registration form.


If I register my team early, will I get to present early during the demo event?
Not necessarily. Demo presentation order wil be rearranged from team sign up order.


What will the demo schedule be like?
Demos begin at 1pm (EST) on Friday, July 18th, at the AMC Empire 25 TheaterWe will have 3 podiums, each with a laptop, from which teams will present. Each laptop will be connected to the VPN and should be able to access dev and QA environments. As each team presents, there will be two other teams “on deck” at the other podiums setting up. . We will have 4 podiums, each with a Macbook Pro laptop, from which teams will present. Each laptop will be connected to the VPN and should be able to access dev and QA environments. As each team presents, there will be three other teams “on deck” at the other podiums setting up. Between demos, we'll annouce who's on deck to get ready, so it's important for you to pay attention and listen for your team to be called.


Can I present my demo from my own laptop?
NO. You MUST use one of the Macbook Pros we provide at the venue to allow a seamless presentation experience for the audience.


How much time will I have to present my Hack?
Each team will have 2 minutes to present their hack project. You are strongly encouraged to practice this presentation.


Will there be prizes?
Each hack will be judged during the off-side demos according to the different categories. Winners of each category will be recognized in some size, shape or form.


Do I need to practice my presentation?
YES! We can't stress this enough! Keep in mind that you will have one chance to present to the judges. The more you practice, the better the presentation will go.


Will there be wifi at the demo venue for me to keep hacking?
The laptops for presenters at the podiums will have relatively fast wired connections and will not be connected to wifi. For everyone else, we will have a shared wifi network at the venue but it probably not be very fast due to the number of people using it. If it looks like wifi is using too much of the bandwidth that presentations get slow, we will have to turn the wifi off.


How will I know when my 2 minutes are up?
Unless you are showing a video, in which case you get 3 minutes total to introduce and play your video. During your team’s presentation, look for the iPad with a timer to see how much time you have left to present. (When there are 30 seconds left, the digits will turn yellow; when there are 10 seconds left, the digits will turn red. If you go overtime, the background will turn red.)


Can I show a video at hackathon?
Yes! In the past we've had teams make funny videos of new features they'd like to see implemented on the site. We've also had remote teams make videos so that they wouldnt have to worry about logistics and bandwidth of doing a presentation. If you are showing a video during your demo, we highly recommend you bring it on a thumb drive or other storage medium rather than trying to stream it over the network. Also, the projector at the movie theater is 4K capable so the higher the resolution of your video the better, but a video at 1080p is fine. All presentation laptops will be Macbook Pros, so please bring a video format compatible to that device. When presenting, make sure to full screen your video on the presentation laptop to provide the best viewing experience for the audience. Don't leave the video playback controls on the screen when it is playing, so please move the mouse pointer away from the controls in QuickTime player and they will disappear after a few seconds.
