Judging Criteria

Only teams who fit the following criteria will be eligible to present and win prizes.
The recommended team size is 4 to 5 people, but you can recruit as many people for your team as you like.

Hack Team Criteria:

  • have at least 4 people from 4 different departments on the team.
  • must build & present a coded prototype.
  • can not enter/win in more than one category.
  • has exactly 2 minutes to present at the semi-final demos.
  • submit your team and demo details by Friday, July 17th at 9:00am local time.

Pitch Team Criteria:

  • must create a video pitch of 2minutes or less
  • have at least 3 people from 3 different departments on the team.
  • can not enter/win in more than one category.
  • has exactly 2 minutes to present at the final throw down on Wednesday, 07/22.
  • submit a completed 2 minute pitch video by Friday, July 17th 9:00am local time (video required, no exceptions).

Departments are defined as:

  • Tech (Developers, Engineers, Tech Ops, QA, BI, IT.)
  • Marketing (Regional Directors, Marketing Design, Blog, PR, ECRM, Events.)
  • Product (Product Managers, UX Designers, Researchers, GMs.)
  • Content (Acquisition, Reviewers, Researchers, Editors, Contributor Support.)
  • Sales & Customer Service (AE's / AM's / SAE's.)
  • Operations (Finance / Legal / HR / Biz Dev.)

Register your team today at the registration page. The deadline for “Hack” and “Pitch” team registration is Friday July 17th at 9:00am EST.

Judging Categories

Hack to the Future is about thinking beyond Shutterstock Inc. as it exists today. When you brainstorm ideas, pitches and hacks with your team, focus on the future. What does the creative process look like 5 years from now? What tools will your team need 3 years from now to be successful? What does success look like for a storyteller?

Here are some resources that might spark inspiration for your hacks and pitches: Internet Trends 2015 - by Mary Meeker. From social networks to market networks - by James Currier on Techcrunch. Marketplaces 2.0 article - by Sangeet Choudary.

Focus your hack or pitch on one of these three categories:

  1. Contributor Impact
  2. Customer Impact
  3. Employee Value

After the Hack demo's finish at the event on Friday, judges in NYC and San Mateo will meet to select the 3 hack semi-finalists from each location. The Pitches will be reviewed by the pitch judges and 3 will be selected to be shown at the final throwdown on Wednesday, July 22nd.

Prizes

Each of the semi-finalists (9 teams) will choose from a selection of subscription services such as Birchbox, Netflix, Amazon and more. The winning team will receive tickets to an event of their choice and their hack/pitch will be considered for a sprint in 2015.
